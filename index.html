PlayStop

A wireless, sound-triggered vibrating armband that empowers hearing-impaired hockey players to independently identify when a whistle has been blown and play has stopped.

↓  See Documentation Learn More

The Project

Empowering Every Ear

PlayStop consists of two custom-built armbands — one for the player and one for the referee. When the referee blows a whistle, the referee's armband detects the specific whistle frequency and wirelessly transmits a signal to the player's armband, triggering a distinct vibration pattern so the hearing-impaired player always knows when play has stopped.


The two armbands communicate via ESP-NOW wireless protocol, using Seeed Studio XIAO ESP32 microcontrollers. The whole system is self-contained, rechargeable, and built to survive the rigors of competitive hockey.

System Overview

How It Works

01

Whistle Detected

The MAX4466 Electret Microphone on the referee's armband continuously monitors ambient sound and captures the referee's whistle frequency.

02

FFT Analysis

The ESP32C3 runs a Fast Fourier Transform on the audio signal to identify whether the detected frequency falls within the pre-set whistle trigger range.

03

Wireless Signal

When a match is confirmed, the referee's armband transmits a signal over ESP-NOW protocol to the player's armband — no Wi-Fi or Bluetooth pairing needed.

04

Vibration Alert

The player's armband vibrates 3 times (1 s on / 0.5 s off) directly against the arm, giving a clear, unmistakable tactile signal that play has stopped.

Hardware

Key Components

🏒

Player Armband

Worn by the hearing-impaired player. Receives the wireless signal and triggers tactile feedback.

  • Seeed Studio XIAO ESP32S3
  • Mini vibratory motor (2N7000 MOSFET driver)
  • 3.7 V rechargeable Li-ion battery
  • 3D-printed protective case + Velcro strap
🎙️

Referee Armband

Worn by the referee on the wrist. Listens for the whistle and transmits the alert signal.

  • Seeed Studio XIAO ESP32C3
  • MAX4466 Electret Microphone
  • 3.7 V rechargeable Li-ion battery
  • 3D-printed protective case + Velcro strap
📡

Wireless Protocol

ESP-NOW enables direct peer-to-peer communication between the two microcontrollers without any network infrastructure.

  • SimpleEspNowConnec library v1.2.0
  • esp32 board package v2.0.11
  • MAC-address based pairing
⚙️

Mechanical Design

Both armbands are built to be durable, adjustable, and easy to maintain in a hockey environment.

  • 0.10 mm layer-height 3D prints (PLA)
  • 23¼" adjustable Velcro straps
  • Sewn fabric pouch for component protection
  • Wi-Fi antenna routed through case wall
